Emotion Recognition
from Speech

The Challenge: Subjective affect

Decoding emotional state from audio waveforms, transcending words to understand paralinguistic "affect".

By Team Emotion Speech | COMP-SCI 5530

Dataset & Approach

Using the **RAVDESS** corpus: 24 professional actors performing eight fundamental emotions.

  • Controlled acoustic environment for clean samples
  • Lexically-matched neutral statements
  • Standardized numerical labeling codebook

Key Technical Methods

Converting raw analog waves into highly dimensional feature matrices for analysis.

  • MFCC Extraction: Audio fingerprints of vocal tracts
  • Data Augmentation: Noise, Pitch-shifting, Time-scaling
  • Models: Deep MLPs and CNNs (Spectrogram images)

Main Results

Evaluating the operating bounds of automated acoustic affect classification.

  • High Arousal Dominance: 80%+ accuracy for Happy/Angry
  • Low Arousal Challenges: Overlap in Neutral/Calm classes
  • Augmentation Impact: Improved generalized performance
